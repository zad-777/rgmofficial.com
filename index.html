About us

Statement of Faith

  • 1. We believe that there is only one God, who has created all things for His glory and honor. This one God manifested Himself to mankind as the father in creation, the Son in redemption. And the Holy Spirit indwelling and inspiring those who place their trust in Him. (Genesis 1:1, Revelation 4:11)

  • 2. We believe that God is love and that sacrificial love was known to fallen humanity when the Father gave the Son to redeem all who believe on the Son.

  • 3. We believe that Jesus Christ was born of the Virgin Mary,lived a sinless human life, willingly took upon Himself all our sins, died and rose again bodily, and is at the right the Father as our advocate and mediator. Someday, He will return to consumnate history and to fulfill the eternal plan of God. (Mat.1:18-32; Mark 16:15-20; Rev. 22:12)

  • 4. We believe that apart from Jesus Christ, all people are spiritually lost and, because of sin deserve the judgement of God. However, God gives salvation and eternal life to anyone who trusts in Jesus Christ and His sacrifice on his or her behalf. Salvation cannot be earned through personal goodness or human effort; instead, it is gifts that must receive by humble repentance and faith in Christ and His finished work on the cross. (Rom.6:23; Eph. 2:8-9)

  • 5. We believe that one must be born again to the Kingdom of God. (John 3:3) 6. We believe that the Bible is God's unique and written revelation to people. It is the inspired, infallible Word of God, and the supreme and final authority on all matters upon which it teaches. No other writings are vested with such authority. (John 1:1-2; 2Tim.3:16-27)

  • 7. We believe in the sanctification through the blood of Jesus Christ in personal holiness of the heart and separation from worldly pressures. (Heb.9:22; Mat.5:8)

  • 8. We believe in the divine healing by faith and that healing in included in the atoning grace of God for man. (Mark 16:17-18; James 5:14)

  • 9. We believe in water baptism as a covenant sign of repentance, at the same time an identification of Jesus death, burial and resurrection. (Mark 16:16; Mat 28:19)

  • 10. We believe in the Holy Spirit baptism and orderly exercising His fruits and gifting. (Acts 1:8; Acts 2:1-4; Cor. 12:4-11)

  • 11. We believe in church relationship with other believers who belong to other fellowships and denomination on the basis that they also accepted and recognized Jesus Christ as their personal Lord and Saviour, and having born again the family of God. (Luke 9:49-50)

  • 12. We believe that civil government is of divine appointment/mandate for the interest and good order of human society and that governors, and rulers should be prayed for, obeyed, and uphold at all times except on things opposed to the will of God, who is the ruler of conscience of His people, "The Kings of Kings and the Lords of Lords". (Rom.13; Acts 5:29; Mat. 23:10)

  • 13. We believe in the intensive evangelizations of the world and missionary work in the accordance with the great commission with signs and wonders following. (Mat.28:19-20; Mark 16:15)

  • 14. We believe that things and offerings are method ordained by God to sustain His ministry and spread the gospel after His command. It is not only a way to take care of the material and financial needs of His church but to raise the spiritual morale of His people to the extent God would abundantly bless them. (Mal 3:8-12; Mat. 22:31)

  • 15. We believe that the discipline within the church is the vital function of the body of Christ. It is necessary to maintain peace, harmony and fellowship. Christ outlined the procedure of administering discipline and this must be followed to all local churches. (Mat. 18:15-17; James 5:20)

  • 16. We believe in the Christian hope the imminent return of Jesus the Messiah. (John 14:1-3; 1 Thes.4:16-17)

  • 17. We believe that Eucharist or the Lord's Supper is to be served often in the church as a commemoration of what Jesus did at the cross of Calvary, in line with a repentant heart, self examination, recognition of God's greatest love and a mutual relationship with the brethren. (1 Cor.11:23-29; Mat.26:28) 18. We believe and recognize church ceremonies like weddings, child dedications, funerals, minister's ordinations, and any other meaningful activities that are designed to enrich Christian life. (Gen.2:24; Mark 10:6-8; Mat.19:13-15)
